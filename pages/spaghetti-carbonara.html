Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti carbonara is a traditional Italian dish consisting of spaghetti, finely chopped pieces of guanciale or pancetta (sometimes substituted with bacon),

egg sauce, pecorino Romano cheese (or a mixture of pecorino Romano and Parmesan), salt and freshly ground black pepper. The dish was invented in the

mid-20th century and is traditionally associated with the Lazio region, especially Rome.
